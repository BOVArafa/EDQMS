## Executive Summary

This proposal presents a strategic consultancy initiative to establish the foundational framework for an **Engineering Team** within Siemens Energy's Power Transformer Repair Business Unit. The engagement focuses on mapping the optimal process for delivering technical offers to the sales department and defining the professional profiles required for this new environment.

By implementing a structured approach, the project aims to create a robust, efficient, and future-ready engineering capability that enhances operational performance and supports Siemens Energy's long-term growth objectives.

---

## 1. Technical Scope

### Project Goal

The primary objective is to develop a comprehensive opportunity map aimed at significantly improving the current offer generation process. This map will serve as the essential blueprint for identifying the key activities that will, in turn, guide the meticulous definition of the competencies and roles required for the new Engineering team.

### Road Map

Given that Siemens Energy operates under a robust quality management system governed by ISO 9001, the optimal methodology for all process analysis is to use this standard as the core directive. This strategy ensures the seamless incorporation of any new process into the company's existing quality framework while enabling a comprehensive review to preemptively identify and document potential non-conformities.

**Phase I: Contextual Analysis and Control**

1. **Market and Organization Context Briefing** — Establishing a thorough understanding of the market dynamics for power transformer repair in the regions where the new Engineering team will operate. This rigorous contextualization is essential for accurately classifying the inherent risks and opportunities that inform the mapping of process activities. Guided by ISO 9001 §4.1.

2. **Quality Management System Analysis** — Assessing the scope and applicability of the company's QMS (ISO 9001 §4.3) to determine the extent to which the offer process should be controlled. Risks and opportunities related to this process and their potential operational impacts will be mapped per §6.1, enabling the identification of key activities within the workflow.

**Phase II: Process Evaluation and Role Definition**

1. **Evaluation of the Offer Process Map** — Identifying and documenting all phases of the process and their principal components in strict adherence to ISO 9001 §4.4. This includes clearly defining the:
   - Inputs and Outputs
   - Sequence and Interactions
   - Required Resources
   - Evaluation Metrics and Measures
   - Opportunities for Improvement

2. **Roles and Profile Definition** — Recognizing that each organizational role demands distinct qualifications, the competencies necessary for effective performance will be precisely defined. In strict alignment with ISO 9001 §7.2, these essential roles will be established based on the standard's definition of competency: a balanced and verifiable combination of education, training, skills, and experience.

### Resources

The successful execution of the defined scope requires the following resources:

**Documentation Access**

Full access to relevant existing information and documentation is critical, including but not limited to:
- **Quality Manual (QM):** For understanding the overall QMS framework and process controls.
- **Current Process Map (As-Is):** Any existing documentation detailing the current repair processes and workflows.
- **Similar Process Maps (New Transformer Production):** Documentation outlining processes for new transformer manufacturing as reference.

**Key Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)**

A significant portion of critical process information resides outside formal documentation. Direct consultation with the following profiles is essential:
- **Offer/Electrical Design Engineer:** For technical specifications, component requirements, and proposal generation.
- **Quality Manager:** For QA checkpoints, non-conformance handling, and compliance requirements.
- **Sales/Commercial Representative:** For the customer journey, scope definition, and service contract processes.
- **Plant/Operations Manager:** For factory layout, resource allocation, and workflow management.

**Software and Tools**

Neun primarily utilizes the Office 365 Professional Suite. Should Siemens Energy mandate the use of specific or proprietary platforms, the client will be responsible for providing the necessary licenses and/or hardware to the consulting team.

### Deliverables

- Optimized Process Workflow Blueprint
- Quality Gate Determination for Workflow
- Role-Specific Recruitment Guidelines
- Go-Forward Strategy Proposal

As **Neun Design** is a tax-domiciled entity in Brazil, the following procedures govern commercial transactions:

- **Contract Currency:** All invoices and payments will be denominated in **Euros (€)**.
