## Executive Summary

This proposal outlines the **second phase** of an engineering improvement initiative aimed at validating a methodology for auditing and developing operational procedures through the application of an **end-to-end engineering process real-world case study**.

The objectives of this phase are to:

- **Validate the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) prototype** developed during the previous Discovery phase
- **Identify improvement opportunities** and define how these enhancements can be effectively implemented

The engagement begins with a **dedicated planning phase (deliverable 001)**, during which a detailed execution schedule will be developed to support the validation of the prototype (deliverable 002). Following the validation activities, the focus will shift to **defining the required improvements (deliverable 003)** and subsequently **designing the implementation approach (deliverable 005)** for the refined solution.

---

## Technical Scope

**Project Goal:** Validate the SOP prototype to determine whether a unified template can handle all variations of activity criteria and confirm its implementation feasibility.

### Road Map

1. **Case Study Selection** — Select one or more real-world projects that best fit the validation criteria *(the selected project must trigger at least 4 processes to provide enough data interactions to validate the SOP template)*.
2. **End-to-End Assessment** — Audit the selected case directly within the production environment to evaluate the live workflow experience.
3. **Performance Evaluation** — Assess whether the prototype can be consistently applied across all processes and activities.
4. **Implementation Assessment** — Evaluate the prototype's readiness for implementation and identify all required improvements.
5. **Solution Definition** — Outline how the identified improvements should be structured to enable effective implementation of the validated prototype.

### Resources Required

- Updated relevant process maps
- Free access to interview stakeholders, process owners, and other actors
