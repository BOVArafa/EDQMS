# Phase 1 — Discovery

The first phase of the EDQMS project was executed under proposal **PRP-C-0016**, a consulting engagement between Neun Design and Siemens Energy's Power Transformer Repair Business Unit. Its purpose was to establish the foundational understanding of the engineering hub's operations and to map the gaps that needed to be addressed before the hub could function effectively.

## Scope

Phase 1 was structured in two sequential stages:

### Stage 1: Contextual Analysis and Control

The first stage focused on understanding the external and internal context in which the hub would operate. This included:

- **Market and organisation context briefing** — mapping the repair market across the regions where the hub would operate, identifying the risks and opportunities that would shape the engineering processes (ISO 9001:2015 §4.1)
- **Quality Management System analysis** — assessing the scope and applicability of Siemens Energy's QMS to determine how much of the offer process needed to be formally controlled, and identifying the key activities within that workflow (ISO 9001:2015 §4.3, §6.1)

### Stage 2: Process Evaluation and Role Definition

The second stage went inside the operations themselves:

- **Offer process mapping** — documenting all phases of the offer generation process and their principal components, including inputs, outputs, sequencing, required resources, evaluation metrics, and improvement opportunities (ISO 9001:2015 §4.4)
- **Role and profile definition** — identifying the competency requirements for each role in the hub, grounded in ISO 9001:2015 §7.2's definition of competency as a verifiable combination of education, training, skills, and experience

## What Was Found

The discovery work revealed three critical gaps in the hub's operational readiness:

### 1. Demand was not substantiated
Business units had projected engineering hours for the hub without a clear rationale. When asked to explain the basis for those projections, they could not. This pointed to a lack of clarity on both sides: the hub did not know what scopes it could offer, and the business units did not know what to request.

### 2. Process maps described a future that did not yet exist
All the process maps presented during the discovery work were designed for an ideal future state. They did not reflect how work was actually being done at the time, nor did they provide guidance on how to execute tasks given current capabilities and constraints. This caused frustration among stakeholders and customers who could not determine what the hub's outputs would be or how to interact with it.

### 3. There was no connection between daily tasks and quality objectives
Neither managers nor operational staff could explain how their day-to-day activities connected to the organisation's requirements and objectives. This was the deepest and most consequential gap — the one that led to the project question.

## The Project Question

The workshop held at the end of Phase 1 brought together stakeholders and actors to map improvement opportunities and define a path forward. After analysing the findings, one question emerged as the framing challenge for everything that followed:

> *"When something happens, will we know when to act, what is required, and how to execute it all?"*

This question became the definition of success for Phase 2.

## Deliverables

| Deliverable | Description | Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Process Risk and Opportunity Assessment Matrix | Maps identified risks and opportunities against the engineering process | Baseline for prioritised action planning |
| Optimised Process Workflow Blueprint | Revised offer process design | Clarifies the sequence and interactions of all process steps |
| Quality Gate Determination | Identifies control points within the workflow | Ensures conformity at critical handover points |
| Role-Specific Recruitment Guidelines | Competency definitions for each engineering role | Enables targeted hiring and performance evaluation |
| Go-Forward Strategy Proposal | Defines the path to Phase 2 | Links discovery findings to the prototype strategy |
